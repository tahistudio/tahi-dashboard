# Homepage redesign — visual reference DNA

Three Dribbble references Liam named, with the specific quality he wants from each.
Translate the FEELING into Tahi's system (Manrope, brand greens, warm-sand canvas,
borders-not-shadows, leaf radius). Never copy the literal style.

## 1. Donezo — Task Management Dashboard  ("I love this poppy design")
https://dribbble.com/shots/25241984-Task-Management-Dashboard

What "poppy" means here, and what to take:
- Friendly confidence. Clean light canvas, then a few SATURATED accent blocks used
  boldly but sparingly. One or two cards are filled with colour and pop; the rest stay
  light and quiet. Not rainbow, not busy, just unafraid of colour where it counts.
- Big, friendly numbers. Large rounded type carries the metrics.
- Soft category colours as pills / chips. Rounded everything.
- Tahi translation: carry "poppy" through the BRAND GREEN. Filled forest-green feature
  tiles that pop against the warm sand; soft brand-tinted chips for status; big confident
  figures in Manrope; the leaf radius as the signature rounded corner. Poppy = one
  confident accent doing bold work + friendly type. NOT a multicoloured card zoo.

## 2. Crextio — HR Management Dashboard  ("white space, unique cards, and card stacking")
https://dribbble.com/shots/25121521-HR-Management-Dashboard-Design

What to take:
- Generous WHITE SPACE. Cards breathe. Nothing is crammed. Air is the luxury signal.
- UNIQUE (non-uniform) cards. Cards vary in size and internal layout; it is not a grid of
  identical tiles. This is the direct antidote to our current "wall of identical cards"
  problem and to impeccable's identical-card-grid ban.
- CARD STACKING. A layered / peeking-card motif: a small stack you flip or page through, or
  a card that peeks out from behind another. Great for a set of like items (upcoming calls,
  announcements, recent deliveries) without spending full-card real estate on each.
- People presence: avatar stacks, a bold dark/feature card anchoring a field of light ones.
- Tahi translation: a true bento of varied card sizes (not a uniform grid); a stacked /
  peeking card for upcoming calls or announcements (page through the next few); avatar
  stacks for Liam + Staci + client contacts; one anchoring feature card (the money or the
  next call) among lighter supporting cards.

## 3. Okisuka — Sales Analytics Dashboard  ("this sort of hierarchy, if that makes sense")
https://dribbble.com/shots/23183454-Okisuka-Sales-Analytics-Dashboard

What "this sort of hierarchy" means, and what to take:
- ONE dominant element, clearly larger than everything else (a hero chart or hero metric),
  with strong size contrast between the headline figure and its supporting context.
- A legible primary -> secondary -> tertiary reading order. The eye knows exactly where to
  land first, then second. Supporting widgets are deliberately sized down and grouped.
- Restrained palette, one accent guiding the eye. Quiet field, loud focal point.
- Tahi translation: this is the fix for "everything is the same visual weight." Establish
  ONE dominant zone (the money, or the day's single most important thing) rendered large at
  the top, then a calm field of smaller supporting cards beneath. Size contrast does the
  hierarchy work; we stop giving 17 surfaces equal weight.
