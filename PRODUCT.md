# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two audiences, one product.

- **Tahi admin (primary for internal surfaces).** Liam (founder, super-admin) and Staci (co-founder). A two-person New Zealand Webflow studio with no other staff. On the admin side they run the entire business: clients, requests, invoices, billing, time, team, capacity, reports, automations, contracts, scheduling, messaging, deals, leads. The admin Overview homepage's real user is Liam glancing at the whole business in a few seconds between calls.
- **Clients (the portal).** Enterprise marketing leaders, Heads of Marketing, and CMOs at Stripe / ElevenLabs / Vercel-tier SaaS (AI, data, healthtech, dev tools, cybersecurity). They ship products where the marketing site has to "look as good as the product and behave like real software." They are time-poor and stressed; attribution is murky; they value craft and visible evidence that real humans are doing real work. In the portal they submit requests, track progress, view invoices, upload files, and message the team, seeing only what is relevant to them.

## Product Purpose

A custom client dashboard built to replace ManyRequests, with task-management depth that rivals a lightweight ClickUp. It is both the daily operating system for the studio and a showcase surface: it must feel like it cost what Tahi charges (a $50k engagement should feel that way the moment a client logs in). Target: production-ready within 6 to 12 months, premium at every screen size including mobile. Success is a portal clients actually adopt instead of reverting to email, and an admin cockpit that lets two founders run an agency without dropping a ball.

## Brand Personality

Crafted, warm, expert. A real New Zealand studio, not a faceless vendor: the leaf mark, Southern-hemisphere soul, real humans visibly doing the work. Calm and premium, restrained but with genuine character. The brand lives where Stripe and Vercel restraint meets Clay and Notion warmth: precise and confident, never cold, never cute. We push back, we go deep over wide, we flag issues before being asked.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS admin panels with no point of view.
- AI slop: walls of identical icon-plus-number-plus-label cards, the hero-metric template, gradient text, a tiny uppercase tracked eyebrow above every section, purple-on-white gradients, evenly-weighted everything.
- ManyRequests / SPP form-field blandness: priority as a dropdown, status as a pill, and nothing more.
- Anything that reads as mass-generated, templated, or cold. If a CMO fluent in Linear, Attio, and Raycast would pause at a subtly-off component, it has failed.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence over promises.** The product proves the retainer. Surface real work happening (live timers, deltas since last visit, edition-numbered deliveries) rather than dashboards of intentions.
2. **Signal over noise.** A stressed founder or CMO reads the important thing in one glance. Hierarchy is ruthless; the money and the one-thing-that-needs-me-now dominate.
3. **Restrained craft with warmth.** Stripe-grade typography and spacing carrying Clay-grade warmth. One accent (brand green) does the work. Borders, not shadows. The tool disappears into the task, then rewards attention with small, earned moments.
4. **Real humans, real place.** New Zealand studio identity is a feature, not decoration: the leaf, two real founders, studio notes, Southern seasons, two clocks. It ages well and never brags about team size.
5. **Earned familiarity.** Behaves like the best tools the audience already trusts. Standard affordances done immaculately beat invented ones. Delight is saved for moments, not sprayed across pages.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA is the target: body text at least 4.5:1, large text at least 3:1, visible focus indicators, semantic HTML and correct ARIA. Reduced motion is honored everywhere via `prefers-reduced-motion` (entrance reveals and count-ups degrade to their final state). Touch targets are at least 44px. Dark mode is first-class (a real four-level elevation system, not an inversion). A Private mode blurs sensitive data so the founder can screen-share the platform without exposing client names or figures.
